Coffee App

When we were asked to design a mobile app for a coffee company of our choosing, my first thought was to create an online ordering application for my friend’s historic tea company.

Although Oliver Pluff & Co. is quite literally steeped in history, I thought the company’s branding could use some updating. It was a ton of fun combining the old and the new on this UI assignment.

Wireframes

In his bestselling book, The Tipping Point, Malcolm Gladwell describes how the best way to make an idea, movement, or product “sticky,” is to make its message interactive in some way.

I knew going into this project that transforming the buying process into a game might encourage users to return to the app more often to purchase Oliver Pluff & Co. products.

Tools Used: Adobe Illustrator

Mockups

Home Screen

Underneath the flavor notes on each product page of the existing Oliver Pluff & Co. website, there’s a short description about that product’s place in history.

Because the products’ historical significance is obviously an integral part of the company’s brand (as well as a source of curiosity and delight for the company’s clientele), it made sense to make these descriptions a more pronounced feature on the product pages of the mobile app.

Prototype

The final step in the process was to import the mockups I had created in Photoshop into InVision and to create hotspots that would lead the user through the mobile app. In doing all of this, I realized I was missing several screens that were necessary to experience the full user journey through the app. I went back and created several more “Haul” or checkout screens and keyboard screens in Photoshop for this reason.

After successfully building a working prototype of the coffee app in InVision, I created an animated version in Principle, which is what you see to the left.

Tools Used: InVision, Photoshop, Principle
